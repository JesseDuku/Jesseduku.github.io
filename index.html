About

Research Focus

I work in water resource engineering, with a focus on hydrologic extremes — floods and droughts — that are growing in frequency and severity under climate variability. My research aims to develop strategies that strengthen community resilience while reducing the impact of these events on marginal populations and the environment.

I am especially interested in compound hazards, where multiple risks unfold simultaneously. By studying these complex interactions, I work to improve our ability to predict and mitigate their combined effects.

A significant part of my work uses statistical and hydrological models to forecast water availability, assess risk, and inform infrastructure design under environmental change. I'm currently exploring how machine learning and AI can transform water resource management with solutions that adapt dynamically to a shifting climate.
