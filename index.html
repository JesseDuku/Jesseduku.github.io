Jesse Duku

Jesse is a Ph.D. researcher in civil engineering at the University of California, Irvine, working on hydrologic extremes, compound hazards, and the role of machine learning in water resource management.

His research investigates floods and droughts under a changing climate — events whose intensity and frequency are reshaping how communities prepare and adapt. He focuses on compound hazards: the interactions between concurrent risks that can amplify damage in ways no single-hazard analysis would predict.

A significant portion of his work uses statistical hydrology and machine learning to forecast water availability, quantify risk, and inform infrastructure that can adapt to environmental change. He's particularly interested in how AI can transform water resource management with tools that respond dynamically to a shifting environment.

Jesse holds a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), where his thesis applied machine learning to predict punching shear failure in concrete slabs — an early thread of the data-driven approach that runs through his work today.
